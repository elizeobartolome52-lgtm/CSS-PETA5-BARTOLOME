"Converge, UP Forge Partnership to Advance AI Education"


The University of the Philippines (UP) and Converge ICT Solutions Inc. have formalized a landmark memorandum of understanding (MoU) to launch a three-year strategic partnership aimed at equipping Filipino students with the skills and leadership needed to thrive in an artificial intelligence-driven global economy.

The ceremonial signing marks a major step forward in the evolution of Philippine higher education toward industry-integrated learning and digital innovation. UP President Angelo A. Jimenez, stated: "Today, we witness not merely a ceremony, but a symphony: the convergence of two great institutions united in purpose and committed to our nation’s digital future. This alliance stands at the threshold of transformation; where academic aspiration meets industrial innovation, where scholarly pursuit embraces technological achievement."

The agreement establishes a framework for collaboration across key technology fields, including artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning, cloud computing, cybersecurity, smart manufacturing, and digital business transformation. It is set to directly benefit UP students enrolled in programs such as AI, Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electronics Engineering, and Business Administration. Dennis Anthony H. Uy, Converge Chief Executive Officer and Co-Founder, said: "his collaboration represents more than skills development. It’s about creating a sustainable pipeline of Filipino technology leaders. Through strategic partnerships like this, we can ensure that Filipino talent not only competes globally, but drives innovation domestically."

Invited as a special guest, Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT) Secretary Henry Aguda commended the initiative as a powerful example of public-private partnership, emphasizing its potential to enhance the government’s digitalization efforts and drive national innovation and development. Structured in three phases, the partnership begins with exploratory initiatives and will deepen its integration by 2026. Phase One introduces the Future Forward Management Trainee Program, a nine-month pilot designed for graduating students. It features intensive training in AI, product development, and digital technology, bridging the gap between academic theory and industry practice that has long challenged Philippine education. Leveraging Converge’s global partnerships with leading technology firms, the collaboration brings an international perspective to the program. UP students will gain exposure to world-class best practices in digitalization and competitiveness while grounding their learning in Filipino values and innovation. Moving beyond traditional internship models, the partnership incorporates new forms of engagement such as AI-focused hackathons, student competitions, and a campus speaker series with industry experts, speed mentoring sessions, and co-developed curricula that fuses AI with business strategy.

A key long-term goal is to establish a Converge Innovation Lab @ UP, a dedicated space for research, prototyping, and real-world AI applications that will serve as a national model for industry-academia cooperation. Peter A. Sy, UP Vice President for Digital Transformation, added: "This partnership demonstrates how strategic academe-industry alliances can accelerate national digital transformation. We’re not just preparing students for existing jobs; we’re empowering them to create new industries and solutions. "

The collaboration also aligns with national priorities in digital transformation and workforce development. Industry studies have highlighted the Philippines’ talent gap in areas such as AI, cybersecurity, and digital business transformation—precisely the competencies this partnership seeks to develop. Beyond skill-building, the MoU includes provisions for social responsibility initiatives aimed at empowering underprivileged yet high-potential students, ensuring inclusive access to digital transformation opportunities. Faculty-industry exchange programs will also be implemented, enabling UP professors to gain practical industry experience while offering Converge access to academic insights and research expertise. Through this initiative, UP and Converge are redefining the relationship between academia and industry. The partnership demonstrates how universities can maintain academic excellence while embracing innovation, with clear guidelines to safeguard academic integrity and institutional values.

Uy concluded: "This represents the future of Philippine higher education. When academic excellence meets industry innovation, we create opportunities that serve both student success and national development. " The three-year agreement includes annual reviews and options for extension, allowing both parties to refine and expand their collaboration as technology and education needs evolve.
